What is GST Return Filing?

GST Return Filing is the mandatory process of reporting your sales, purchases, tax collected, and tax paid to the Indian Government. It is the cornerstone of GST compliance for every registered entity.

Compliance & ITC

Ensure legal compliance, avoid penalties, and correctly claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) to manage cash flow.

Mandatory For

All registered entities, including regular taxpayers, composition dealers, e-commerce operators, and TDS deductors.

Filing Frequency

Returns are filed monthly, quarterly, or annually, based on the taxpayer's registration type and aggregate turnover.

Key GST Return Forms

Different GST forms serve distinct purposes, from detailing sales and summarizing liabilities to annual reconciliation.

Form Frequency Purpose Applicable For
GSTR-1 Monthly / Quarterly Detailed report of all sales (outward supplies). All regular taxpayers
GSTR-3B Monthly Summary return for calculating and paying GST liability. All regular taxpayers
CMP-08 Quarterly Quarterly statement and tax payment form. Composition dealers
GSTR-5 Monthly Return for Non-Resident Foreign Taxable Person. Non-Resident Taxpayers
GSTR-6 Monthly Return for distributing input tax credit. Input Service Distributors (ISD)
GSTR-7 Monthly Return for authorities deducting tax at source (TDS). TDS Deductors
GSTR-9 Annually Consolidated annual summary of all GST transactions. Regular taxpayers
GSTR-10 Once Final return filed upon cancellation of GST registration. Taxpayers cancelling GSTIN

The GST Filing Workflow

Follow this systematic process on the official GST portal to ensure accurate and timely filing of your returns.

Step 1: Login & Prepare GSTR-1

Log in to the GST portal and navigate to the Return Dashboard. Meticulously compile and upload all sales invoices, credit notes, and debit notes for the period.

Step 2: Validate GSTR-1 and Submit

Verify the summarized details, ensure accuracy of all entries, generate the summary, and then submit and file GSTR-1 using DSC or EVC.

Step 3: Verify ITC in GSTR-2B

After the due date, GSTR-2B (a static ITC statement) will be generated. Download it to reconcile your purchase records with the ITC available to you.

Step 4: Prepare & File GSTR-3B with Payment

GSTR-3B auto-populates data from GSTR-1. Enter the eligible ITC from GSTR-2B, calculate the net tax liability, make the payment via cash/credit ledgers, and file the return.

Required Documentation
  • Sales and Purchase Invoices (B2B and B2C)
  • Issued Credit / Debit Notes
  • HSN / SAC wise summary of sales and purchases
  • Bank statements for payment reconciliation
  • E-way Bills generated during the period
Data from GST Portal
  • Auto-drafted GSTR-2B for Input Tax Credit matching
  • Electronic Cash, Credit, and Liability Ledgers
  • Previously filed GST returns for reference

Compliance: Penalties & ITC Rules

Understanding the financial consequences of non-compliance and the strict conditions for claiming Input Tax Credit is crucial for every business.

Late Fees & Interest
  • Late Fee: A statutory fee of ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) is levied for late filing of GSTR-1 & 3B. Reduced fees apply for NIL returns.
  • Interest: Interest at 18% per annum is charged on the net tax liability for any delay in payment.
Persistent delays can lead to GST registration cancellation and other legal actions.
Key ITC Claim Conditions
  • ITC can only be claimed if you have a valid tax invoice or debit note.
  • You must have received the specified goods or services.
  • The supplier must have paid the tax to the government and filed their GSTR-1.
  • The specific credit must be visible in your GSTR-2B statement.
  • You must pay your supplier within 180 days from the invoice date.
