TDS & TCS Filing Guide

Understand Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) and Tax Collected at Source (TCS).

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)

TDS is a system where the person responsible for making specified payments (like salary, rent, professional fees) deducts a certain percentage of tax before making the full payment to the receiver.

Common TDS Sections
SectionNature of PaymentRate
192SalarySlab Rates
194AInterest Income10%
194CPayment to Contractors1% (Ind/HUF), 2% (Others)
194IRent2% (P&M), 10% (Land/Building)
194JProfessional/Technical Fees10%
How It Works: An Example

A company pays a consultant a professional fee of ₹1,00,000.

  • TDS Deduction: The company deducts 10% TDS (under Sec 194J) = ₹10,000.
  • Net Payment: The consultant receives ₹1,00,000 - ₹10,000 = ₹90,000.
  • Deposit & Credit: The company deposits the ₹10,000 with the government, and the consultant can claim this amount as a tax credit when filing their ITR.

Tax Collected at Source (TCS)

TCS is a tax collected by the seller from the buyer at the time of selling certain goods. The seller is responsible for collecting the tax and depositing it with the government.

Common TCS Provisions
SectionNature of Goods/TransactionRate
206C(1)Sale of Alcoholic Liquor, Tendu Leaves, Scrap1% - 5%
206C(1F)Sale of Motor Vehicle1% (if value > ₹10 Lakh)
206C(1H)Sale of Goods (any)0.1% (if turnover > ₹10 Cr & sale > ₹50 Lakh)
How It Works: An Example

A car dealer sells a car for ₹15,00,000.

  • TCS Collection: The dealer collects 1% TCS (as value > ₹10 Lakh) = ₹15,000.
  • Total Payment: The buyer pays ₹15,00,000 + ₹15,000 = ₹15,15,000.
  • Deposit & Credit: The dealer deposits the ₹15,000 with the government, and the buyer can claim this amount as a tax credit.

Key Differences: TDS vs. TCS

BasisTDSTCS
MeaningTax is deducted from income/payment.Tax is collected over and above the sale price.
Trigger PointOn payment of expenses like salary, rent, etc.On sale of specified goods.
ResponsibilityPayer/DeductorSeller/Collector
ApplicabilityOn payments exceeding specified limits.On sales exceeding specified limits.
