Import & Export (IE) Code Guide

A comprehensive guide to the IE Code and the import/export process in India.

Basic Setup IEC Code AD Code & RCMC Export Process Import Process Key Laws

1. Basic Business Setup

These preliminary steps are required before initiating any import or export activities.

  • Business & Tax Registration

    • Register your business as a Proprietorship, Partnership, LLP, or Private Limited Company.
    • Obtain a Permanent Account Number (PAN) for the business entity.
    • Secure GST registration, which is essential for goods trading.

  • Bank Account

    Open a current account with a bank authorized to deal in foreign exchange (an AD Bank).

2. Importer-Exporter Code (IEC)

A mandatory 10-digit code issued by the Director General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) for the import and export of goods.

  • Online Application on DGFT Portal

    Register on the DGFT website (dgft.gov.in), fill out the online application with your business, PAN, and bank details, upload the required documents, and pay the fee to get your IEC instantly.

3. AD Code & RCMC

  • Authorised Dealer (AD) Code

    A 14-digit code provided by your bank. It must be registered with customs at each port of export to enable direct crediting of foreign currency transactions and benefits.

  • Registration-cum-Membership Certificate (RCMC)

    Issued by Export Promotion Councils (EPCs), this certificate is required to claim various benefits and concessions under the Foreign Trade Policy.

4. The Export Process

Key Stages:

  1. Pre-Shipment: Secure an order, arrange goods, and prepare documents like the commercial invoice and packing list.
  2. Customs Clearance: File a Shipping Bill on the ICEGATE portal. Goods are inspected and cleared by customs, after which a "Let Export Order" is given.
  3. Post-Shipment: Submit documents to your bank for payment realization. Once payment is received, apply for any eligible export incentives (e.g., GST refund).

5. The Import Process

Key Stages:

  1. Pre-Shipment: Check import policies for your product, obtain necessary licenses, and place the order with the foreign supplier.
  2. Shipment & Documentation: The supplier ships the goods and provides key documents like the Bill of Lading (for sea) or Air Waybill (for air).
  3. Customs Clearance: File a Bill of Entry on the ICEGATE portal. Pay the applicable customs duties and IGST. After clearance, customs issues an "Out of Charge" order.
  4. Post-Clearance: Take delivery of the goods and claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) for the IGST paid, if eligible.

6. Key Governing Laws

  • Foreign Trade (Development & Regulation) Act, 1992: The primary law governing foreign trade, managed by the DGFT.
  • Customs Act, 1962: Governs the levy of duties and procedures for import/export clearance.
  • Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999: Regulates payments and foreign exchange, managed by the RBI.
  • GST Laws: Govern the levy of IGST on imports and refunds for exports.
