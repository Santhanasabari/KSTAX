What is a Digital Signature Certificate?

A DSC is an electronic signature that verifies identity and secures documents, with legal validity under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

Legally valid Secure Faster approvals
Where DSC is used

Common processes and portals that accept or require DSCs.

Corporate Filings
MCA portal – company registration, annual returns, LLP forms.
Tax & GST
Income Tax e-filing, GST return signing and authorisations.
Professional Certification
CAs, CSs, and advocates use DSCs for certifying and filing documents.
e-Procurement & Tenders
Bidding on government portals, GeM, and e-tender systems.
Types of DSC
Class 2
Used for filing in government portals and certifying documents.
Class 3
Higher assurance, used for e-tenders and sensitive filings.
Signing & Encryption
Separate certificates for signing or encrypting data.

Sample Documents & Notes
  • Identity Proof: PAN, Aadhaar, Passport copy (scanned)
  • Address Proof: Aadhaar / Passport / Utility bill
  • Applicant Photo: Passport size
  • Organisation Documents: Certificate of Incorporation, Board Resolution (if company)

For e-tendering, Class 3 DSC is recommended. For general filings Class 2 may be sufficient depending on portal rules.
